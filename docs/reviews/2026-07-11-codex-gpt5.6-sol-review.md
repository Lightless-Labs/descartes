# Consolidated review report

Verdict: **not safe to ship under the stated invariants**. The deterministic core is promising, but three safety blockers remain. No repository files were modified.

## Blockers

### 1. Raw provenance and constraint data can reach the LLM

The sanitization boundary does not cover all outward-facing fields.

- Constraint `id`, `family`, and `target` are interpolated directly into `fingerprint`, `title`, and `summary`; only `diagnostics` is sanitized: [constraint-eval.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/constraint-eval.js:67). `normalizeAlertRecord` then preserves those strings without bounding or sanitizing them: [alert-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/alert-store.js:161).
- The alert-intelligence prompt copies `title`, `summary`, and diagnostics directly: [alert-intelligence.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/alert-intelligence.js:144), [alert-intelligence.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/alert-intelligence.js:181).
- `inspect_runtime_provenance` is unconditionally model-visible: [pi-harness.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/pi-harness.js:190). Its result includes raw executable paths, process commands, usernames, and ancestry command snippets: [provenance.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/tools/provenance.js:605), [provenance.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/tools/provenance.js:614). The complete envelope is serialized into the tool result: [pi-harness.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/pi-harness.js:29).
- Constraint validation permits arbitrary nonempty strings and permits `status:"active"`; it does not enforce the sanitizer’s safe-string rules: [constraint-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/constraint-store.js:34).

Why it matters: an adversarial or hand-authored constraint can place a path, username, command line, or prompt injection into alerts and the background LLM prompt. The provenance tool independently sends raw identity information during triage. This directly violates the requested “hashed or sanitized before alert/notification/LLM” invariant.

### 2. The nonce gate is not the only enforceable activation path, and approval is non-atomic

The authority boundary is convention-based rather than structurally enforced.

- `writeConstraints()` is exported and accepts any valid status, including `active`: [constraint-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/constraint-store.js:118).
- `promoteReviewReadyToActive()` is also exported and requires no promotion record or nonce: [constraint-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/constraint-store.js:383).
- Four shipped seed records are already `active` without going through a nonce: [constraint-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/constraint-store.js:286).
- Approval writes `constraints.json` first and `promotions.json` second, without locking or a transaction: [promotion-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/promotion-store.js:389). A crash after the first write leaves an active constraint with a pending approval. Concurrent approve/reject calls can both read the same pending state and race to last-writer-wins outcomes.
- Attempts with no matching promotion record are not audited because `logDenial()` returns without writing: [promotion-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/promotion-store.js:271).
- The “single writer” tests merely search for literals in selected source slices; they do not prove call-graph exclusivity, serialization, or atomicity: [promotion-store.test.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/test/promotion-store.test.js:517), [constraint-store.test.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/test/constraint-store.test.js:431).

Why it matters: the load-bearing “only explicit, single-use, audited approval can activate” claim is false as an API property and unsafe under crashes or concurrent CLI invocations.

### 3. Cross-UID provenance does not consistently degrade

Linux unresolved port ownership has the intended partial shape, but direct PID and macOS port paths do not enforce it.

- `resolvePidCore()` performs no own-UID comparison. Any process present in `ps` is returned with `resolvedStatus:"ok"`, PID, UID/username and ancestry, even if executable inspection failed: [provenance.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/tools/provenance.js:587), [provenance.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/tools/provenance.js:614).
- The macOS port path takes the first `lsof` PID and delegates to that same unrestricted resolver: [provenance.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/tools/provenance.js:643).
- An `ok` classified result becomes confidence `1`: [provenance.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/tools/provenance.js:456).
- By contrast, the Linux unresolved/cross-UID port branch correctly leaves PID undefined and returns `partial`/`missing_permission`: [provenance.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/tools/provenance.js:687).
- The S5 identity gatherer also correctly filters to positively confirmed own-UID processes: [provenance-identity.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/tools/provenance-identity.js:118).

Additionally, both macOS and Linux port resolution select the first matching socket/owner while presenting all same-port sockets under that provenance result: [provenance.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/tools/provenance.js:647), [provenance.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/tools/provenance.js:675). `SO_REUSEPORT`, dual-stack, or multiple-owner cases should be ambiguous, not attributed to the first PID.

Why it matters: cross-UID or multi-owner targets can be returned as confident, singular provenance and sent to the model, violating degrade-not-fabricate.

## Major findings

### 4. Shadow promotion ignores fires after the first fixed soak window

`checkShadowSoak()` examines only records from `shadowSince` up to `shadowSince + soakWindow`, regardless of when promotion is attempted: [constraint-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/constraint-store.js:213), [constraint-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/constraint-store.js:229).

A constraint can have a clean first seven days, fire repeatedly on days 8–10, and still promote when `learned soak` is finally run on day 10. This contradicts the module’s own claim that one fire leaves it shadow indefinitely. The gate should examine a contiguous window ending at promotion time or reject any fire since shadow enrollment.

### 5. Identity alerts never age out when a process or bind disappears

Reconciliation only folds observations into existing signatures; it does not mark unseen records inactive or retired: [provenance-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/provenance-store.js:307). Candidate derivation emits for every historical `known_good/grace_window` record and every historical port key without checking current presence or `last_seen` age: [provenance-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/provenance-store.js:466).

Consequences:

- `unknown_identity` remains active after the process exits.
- `new_public_bind` remains active after the socket closes.
- Old identity pairs can keep `identity_drift` active indefinitely.

This defeats alert recovery semantics. There are no disappearance/recovery tests.

Stat failures compound this: the gatherer still emits an observation with `identityHash:undefined`: [provenance-identity.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/tools/provenance-identity.js:137). Repeated permission or TOCTOU failures can therefore stabilize as a distinct identity and create false unknown/drift alerts.

### 6. Linux provenance scanning is not genuinely bounded, and the deadline does not cancel it

The PID count is capped at 4096, but every FD of every selected PID is enumerated and read sequentially with no per-process or total FD cap: [provenance.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/tools/provenance.js:307), [provenance.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/tools/provenance.js:315). The S4 collector invokes that full scan: [provenance-warnings.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/tools/provenance-warnings.js:316).

S5 then independently repeats listener/PID resolution during identity reconciliation: [provenance-identity.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/tools/provenance-identity.js:170).

The daemon deadline only abandons the promise; it explicitly leaves collection running: [daemon.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/daemon.js:223). Thus a timed-out scan can continue consuming I/O after the tick completes.

Why it matters: high-FD servers can incur very long, duplicated background scans. This also places L0 host I/O and store mutation inside an L1 candidate-computation function, weakening layer separation.

### 7. The single kill switch is not actually global, and learned alerts can enter existing LLM adjudication

The implementation follows the plan’s narrower “automatic work only” interpretation, not the review brief’s “entire subsystem inert” requirement.

- `learned mine` intentionally ignores `learned.json`: [constraint-miner.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/constraint-miner.js:280).
- `soak`, `review`, `approve`, `reject`, and provenance snapshot similarly do not load it: [shadow-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/shadow-store.js:294), [provenance-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/provenance-store.js:534).
- The model-visible provenance tool is always registered.
- All due alerts, including learned namespaces, are sent to existing alert intelligence: [daemon.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/daemon.js:445). Alert-intelligence config has no `enabled_namespaces` or learned/provenance re-consent field: [alert-intelligence.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/alert-intelligence.js:23).

The LLM route remains explicit, rate-limited and audited, so this is not an unbounded hidden LLM. However, an operator who previously enabled intelligence for metric alerts automatically consents to new learned/provenance alert payloads once `learned.json` is enabled. That contradicts the roadmap’s promised per-namespace re-consent.

### 8. Target truncation can merge unrelated constraints

The miner sanitizes a 64-character entity key, prefixes the fact name, then sanitizes/truncates the combined target to 64 characters again: [constraint-miner.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/constraint-miner.js:111). The lookup reconstructs targets using the same truncation: [shadow-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/shadow-store.js:200).

Two long service names sharing the same prefix can therefore have different hash-derived constraint IDs but the same evaluation target. “Latest wins” then evaluates both against one fact. Long IPv6 socket identities are also collision-prone.

Targets should include a fixed-length hash of the full entity identity, not depend on prefix truncation.

### 9. “Service presence” cannot detect disappearance

The translator emits facts only for services included in the current collector output: [fact-translators.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/fact-translators.js:25). The collector itself truncates to 80 entries: [services.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/tools/services.js:145). If a service disappears or falls outside the limit, no `running:false` fact is produced. Evaluation treats a missing fact as “no claim” and skips it: [constraint-eval.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/constraint-eval.js:99).

Thus the family detects some running-state flips, not service presence loss. On Linux, port-binding identity mining is also effectively unavailable because the base network collector supplies no owner; this limitation is documented but materially narrows Layer A.

## Minor findings

- `loadLearnedConfig()` throws on malformed JSON rather than failing closed to disabled, so a corrupt config can crash daemon iterations: [constraint-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/constraint-store.js:144).
- The daemon reads `learned.json` independently for structural work and each candidate source. A concurrent config change can produce a mixed-source tick and spurious recovery. One config snapshot should govern the whole iteration: [daemon.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/daemon.js:352), [daemon.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/daemon.js:436).
- The executable “identity hash” is a stat tuple, not content identity. Same-inode/same-size/preserved-mtime replacement remains invisible: [provenance-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/provenance-store.js:128). This is documented in code, but commit/handoff language still overstates “same-path binary swap” coverage.
- Documentation is stale: `docs/HANDOFF.md` still says the identity hash is absent, while HEAD now computes the stat fingerprint: [HANDOFF.md](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/docs/HANDOFF.md:22). Its header date is also older than its session entries.
- There is no user-facing `learned enable/disable/status` command despite the switch being operationally load-bearing; users must create `learned.json` manually.

## Invariants that do hold

- **Read-only host behavior:** the new collectors use reads/stat/readlink and fixed-argv inspection commands. Learned writes are confined to Descartes config/state stores.
- **No privilege escalation:** no `sudo`, `setcap`, privileged helper, administrator AppleScript, `shell:true`, or elevated daemon path exists in the reviewed implementation. S3-priv is genuinely absent.
- **No direct LLM dependency in miner/shadow/promotion/provenance candidate modules:** those modules remain deterministic. The caveat is the downstream generic alert-intelligence route described above.
- **Automatic draft/shadow paths stop at review-ready:** mining emits drafts, shadow evaluation has a distinct non-alert shape, and soak does not directly activate.
- **Single alert merge:** all learned candidate sources are concatenated before one `applyAlertCandidates()` call: [daemon.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/daemon.js:426), [alert-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/alert-store.js:271). The no-cross-source-recovery design is sound.
- **Fact storage:** retention and max-size enforcement, corrupt-line tolerance, and atomic retention rewrites are sensible: [fact-store.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/fact-store.js:109).
- **Day-1 identity no-storm:** identity candidates are suppressed until an explicit snapshot establishes `bootstrapped_at`: [provenance-identity.js](/Users/thomas/Projects/lightless-labs/public/descartes/tools/descartes-cli/src/tools/provenance-identity.js:170).

## Test assessment

The suite contains meaningful functional tests for normal nonce/expiry behavior, shadow candidate separation, corrupt stores, UID filtering, kill-switch short circuits, exact hash serialization, and cross-source alert recovery.

Safety-critical gaps remain:

- adversarial constraint title/summary/fingerprint sanitization;
- direct-PID and macOS cross-UID degradation;
- multi-owner same-port attribution;
- concurrent approve/approve and approve/reject races;
- failure between the two approval-store writes;
- a shadow fire after the initial soak window;
- process/socket disappearance and identity alert recovery;
- transient missing `identityHash`;
- long-name target collisions;
- actual cancellation after structural deadline;
- learned-namespace LLM consent;
- an end-to-end assertion that no raw provenance identity reaches a model prompt.

The source-regex “single writer” and “no LLM import” tests are particularly weak: they prove spelling/import placement, not authority, call-graph reachability, concurrency safety, or downstream prompt routing.
